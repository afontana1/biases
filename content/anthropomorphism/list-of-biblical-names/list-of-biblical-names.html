Names play a variety of roles in the Bible. They sometimes relate to the nominee's role in a biblical narrative, as in the case of Nabal, a foolish man whose name means "fool".[1] Names in the Bible can represent human hopes, divine revelations, or are used to illustrate prophecies.[2][clarification needed]

Most popular names[edit]

During the period 330 BCE — 200 CE, the most common male names in Palestine were:[3]

  • 1. Simon/Simeon
  • 2. Joseph/Joses
  • 3. Lazarus
  • 4. Judas
  • 5. John
  • 6. Jesus (Yeshua)
  • 7. Ananias
  • 8. Jonathan
  • 9. Matthew/Matthias
  • 10. Manaen
  • 11. James

During this period, the most common female names were:[3]

  • 1. Mary
  • 2. Salome
  • 3. Shelamzion
  • 4. Martha
  • 5-6. Joanna & Sapphira (equally common)
  • 7. Berenice
  • 8-9. Imma & Mara (equally common)
  • 10-12. Cyprus & Sarah & Alexandra
